CONDUCT

G201 -- Be a good person.
Description Follow the FIRST Code of Conduct.
Penalty Verbal Warning Yellow Card if Subsequent during event
G202 -- DRIVE TEAM interactions.
Description A human may only enter the FIELD during pre-MATCH to setup their ROBOT and after a MATCH is over to stop and collect their ROBOT after being instructed to do so by the Head REFEREE (or designee).
Penalty Verbal Warning Yellow Card if Subsequent during event
G203 -- Asking other teams to throw a MATCH – not cool.
Description A team may not encourage an ALLIANCE of which it is not a member to play beneath its ability.
Penalty Verbal Warning Red Card if Subsequent during event
G204 -- Letting someone coerce you into throwing a MATCH.
Description A team, as the result of encouragement by a team not on their ALLIANCE, may not play beneath its ability.
Penalty Verbal Warning Red Card if subsequent during event
G205 -- Throwing your own MATCH is bad.
Description A team may not intentionally lose a MATCH or sacrifice RANKING POINTS to lower their own ranking or manipulate another team’s ranking.
Penalty Verbal Warning Red Card if subsequent during event
G206 -- Don't violate rules for RPs.
Description Teams or ALLIANCES may not collude to intentionally violate a rule in an attempt to influence RANKING POINTS.
Penalty Yellow Card ALLIANCE ineligible for PATTERN and GOAL RPs
G207 -- Do not abuse ARENA access.
Description Non-DRIVE TEAM members with special access (e.g., media badges) may not assist, coach, or signal during a MATCH. Safety exceptions only.
Penalty Verbal Warning Yellow Card if subsequent during event
G208 -- Show up to your MATCHES.
Description If a ROBOT has passed inspection, at least one DRIVE TEAM member must report to the ARENA and participate in each assigned Qualification MATCH.
Penalty Disqualified from the current MATCH
G209 -- Keep your ROBOT together.
Description A ROBOT may not intentionally detach or leave any part on the FIELD.
Penalty Red Card
G210 -- Do not expect to gain by doing others harm.
Description Actions aimed at forcing the opponent ALLIANCE to violate a rule are not allowed. The targeted ALLIANCE will not receive a penalty.
Penalty Minor Foul Major Foul if repeated No penalty assigned to targeted ALLIANCE
G211 -- Egregious or exceptional violations.
Description Egregious behavior or repeated violations of any rule or procedure may result in a CARD at any time during the event, assigned by the Head REFEREE.
Penalty Yellow Card Red Card (if severe)
G212 -- All teams can play.
Description A team may not encourage another team to exclude their ROBOT or get DISQUALIFIED from a Qualification MATCH for any reason.
Penalty Yellow Card Red Card if the ROBOT does not participate

PRE-MATCH

G301 -- Be prompt.
Description A DRIVE TEAM member may not cause significant delays to the start of their MATCH. Delay occurs when the scheduled start time has passed and the team, with ARENA access, is not ready or making a good-faith effort to be ready.
Penalty Verbal Warning Major Foul if subsequent within tournament phase Robot Disabled if not ready within 2 minutes and no good faith effort
Extra Consult the manual for details on Qualification vs. Playoff handling.
G302 -- Limit what you bring to the FIELD.
Description Items brought to the FIELD, beyond the ROBOT and OPERATOR CONSOLE, must fit in the ALLIANCE AREA, be worn/held by DRIVE TEAM, or be an accommodation item. Items must not create hazards, exceed 6'6", communicate externally, block visibility, or interfere with the ARENA.
Penalty Match will not start until situation is remedied Yellow Card if discovered or used inappropriately during MATCH
G303 -- ROBOTS on the FIELD must come ready to play.
Description ROBOTS must be safe, inspected, compliant with any modifications, the only team-provided item on the FIELD, show correct ALLIANCE color, and remain motionless after OpMode initialization.
Penalty Match will not start until requirements met (if quick remedy) Disabled if not quick remedy; Red Card if uninspected or noncompliant ROBOT participates
Extra Head REFEREE may require re-inspection before next MATCH.
G304 -- ROBOTS must be set up correctly on the FIELD.
Description ROBOTS must start over a LAUNCH LINE, touching their GOAL or FIELD perimeter, fully contained on their side, not entangled or attached, in STARTING CONFIGURATION, and within allowed preload limits.
Penalty Match will not start until setup corrected (if quick remedy) Disabled if not a quick remedy
Extra ROBOTS must be fully contained within FIELD perimeter wall.
G305 -- Teams must select an OpMode.
Description Teams must select and initialize an OpMode on the DRIVER STATION app before the MATCH. AUTO OpModes must have the 30-second AUTO timer enabled.
Penalty Match will not start until situation remedied Disabled if OpMode cannot initialize or issue not quickly fixed

AUTO

G401 -- Let the ROBOT do its thing.
Description Once FIELD STAFF begins randomization and until AUTO ends, DRIVE TEAM members may not interact with their ROBOT or OPERATOR CONSOLE except to: (A) press start at the MATCH start, (B) press stop when directed or at their discretion, or (C) ensure personal or console safety.
Penalty Major Foul ALLIANCE ineligible for PATTERN points in AUTO if ARTIFACT enters GOAL after interaction
G402 -- No AUTO opponent interference.
Description During AUTO, ROBOTS must stay on their side of the FIELD (columns A–C for blue, D–F for red). A ROBOT may not contact an opponent fully on its side or disturb pre-staged ARTIFACTS on the opposing side, directly or indirectly (including through ARTIFACT contact or launches).
Penalty Major Foul per instance of ROBOT contact (G402.A) Major Foul per ARTIFACT disrupted (G402.B)

TELEOP

G403 -- ROBOTS are motionless between AUTO and TELEOP.
Description Any powered movement of the ROBOT or its MECHANISMS is not allowed during the transition period between AUTO and TELEOP.
Penalty Major Foul
Notes
  • Movement caused by inertia, gravity, or actuator de-energizing is not a violation.
  • Teams may stop AUTO or initialize TELEOP during the transition, but INIT must not cause motion. If it does, wait until TELEOP starts.
  • Strategic violations (e.g., launching elements, operating the gate, or significant robot motion) are considered egregious behavior under G211.
G404 -- ROBOTS are motionless at the end of TELEOP.
Description ROBOTS must stop all powered movement after TELEOP ends until the Head REFEREE or designee signals retrieval is allowed.
Penalty Minor Foul Major Foul per ARTIFACT launched into GOAL after TELEOP Major Foul if ROBOT contacts GATE after TELEOP
Notes
  • Movement from inertia, gravity, or actuator de-energizing is not powered movement.
  • DRIVE TEAMS should clearly show the ROBOT is no longer controlled by pressing the stop (■) button.

SCORING ELEMENTS

G405 -- ROBOTS use SCORING ELEMENTS as directed.
Description A ROBOT may not deliberately use a SCORING ELEMENT to make a FIELD challenge easier or harder in a way not intended by the game design.
Penalty Major Foul per SCORING ELEMENT
Notes
  • Intentionally positioning SCORING ELEMENTS to block opponent access to FIELD elements.
  • Placing SCORING ELEMENTS into inaccessible areas (e.g., under the RAMP or GOAL).
  • Using SCORING ELEMENTS to prop open the GATE.
G406 -- Keep SCORING ELEMENTS in bounds.
Description A ROBOT may not intentionally eject a SCORING ELEMENT from the FIELD, whether directly or by bouncing off another object.
Penalty Major Foul per SCORING ELEMENT
Notes SCORING ELEMENTS that leave the FIELD during normal scoring attempts are not considered intentional ejections.
G407 -- Do not damage SCORING ELEMENTS.
Description Neither ROBOTS nor DRIVE TEAM members may damage SCORING ELEMENTS during play or handling.
Penalty Verbal Warning Major Foul if repeated Disabled if ROBOT damage likely to recur
Notes Corrective action (e.g., removing or modifying damaging mechanisms) and reinspection may be required before the ROBOT competes again.
G408 -- No more than 3 at a time.
Description A ROBOT may not simultaneously CONTROL more than three (3) ARTIFACTS.
Penalty Minor Foul per SCORING ELEMENT over the limit Yellow Card if excessive
Notes
  • Not considered CONTROL: bulldozing, deflecting, or incidental contact while collecting.
  • LAUNCHED elements no longer in contact with the ROBOT are not controlled.
  • Excessive control = 5+ ARTIFACTS at once or frequent violations (3+ occurrences with 4+ ARTIFACTS).
  • Design ROBOTS to prevent unintentional control above the limit.

ROBOT

G409 -- ROBOTS must be under control.
Description A ROBOT must not pose an undue hazard to humans or ARENA elements. This includes disrupting anything or contacting a person outside the FIELD, or operating in a dangerous manner.
Penalty Disabled Verbal Warning Yellow Card if repeated or subsequent during event
Notes
  • Be aware of REFEREES and FIELD STAFF near the ARENA.
  • Examples: flailing outside the FIELD, knocking over a DRIVER STATION stand, moving/damaging timer display, or contacting FIELD STAFF.
  • Contact with elements outside the FIELD perimeter (e.g., DRIVER STATION stand or floor) is not a violation.
G410 -- ROBOTS must stop when instructed.
Description If instructed to DISABLE by a REFEREE, a DRIVE TEAM member must immediately press the stop (■) button on the DRIVER STATION app.
Penalty Major Foul for greater-than-momentary delay Red Card if continuous
G411 -- ROBOTS must be identifiable.
Description A ROBOT’s team number and ALLIANCE color must remain clear and identifiable at all times, as determined by the Head REFEREE.
Penalty Verbal Warning Minor Foul if subsequent during event
G412 -- Don’t damage the FIELD.
Description ROBOTS may not damage FIELD elements during play.
Penalty Verbal Warning Disabled if further damage likely Yellow Card for any subsequent damage during event
Notes Corrective action (e.g., removing or modifying damaging mechanisms) and reinspection may be required before competing again. G412 does not apply to damage caused by normal gameplay actions.
G413 -- Watch your ARENA interaction.
Description ROBOTS may not grab, grasp, attach to, entangle with, or suspend from ARENA elements, except for permitted SCORING ELEMENT interactions (see G407).
Penalty Major Foul Yellow Card if repeated or greater-than-momentary Disabled if further damage likely
Notes
  • Corrective action (removal or modification of mechanism, reinspection) may be required.
  • ROBOTS operating the GATE should push the lever down to open; pulling or applying closing force violates this rule.
G414 -- ROBOTS have horizontal expansion limits.
Description ROBOTS must comply with horizontal expansion limits per R105.A during the MATCH. Over-expansion due to damage is permitted if not used strategically.
Penalty Minor Foul Major Foul if used for strategic benefit or affects scoring
G415 -- ROBOTS have vertical expansion limits (with exceptions).
Description ROBOTS must comply with vertical expansion limits per R105. Expansion above 18 in. (45.7 cm) up to 38 in. (96.5 cm) is allowed only during the final 20 seconds of the MATCH and when not in any LAUNCH ZONE.
Penalty Minor Foul Major Foul if used for strategic benefit or affects scoring
G416 -- LAUNCHING in the LAUNCH ZONE only.
Description ROBOTS may only LAUNCH SCORING ELEMENTS when inside a LAUNCH ZONE or overlapping a LAUNCH LINE.
Penalty Minor Foul per launched SCORING ELEMENT Major Foul per launched SCORING ELEMENT if it enters open top of GOAL
Notes
  • “LAUNCHING” includes shooting, propelling, or forcefully throwing SCORING ELEMENTS.
  • “Bulldozing” or short-distance expulsion during normal operation is not LAUNCHING.
  • Intent is not to penalize manipulators expelling elements through standard play (e.g., intake reversal).
G417 -- ROBOTS only operate GATES as directed.
Description ROBOTS may not:
  • A. contact, directly or transitively, an opposing Alliance's gate
  • B. apply, either directly or transitively, any closing force to either gate
    • NOTE: CONSIDER G418
    Penalty Major Foul) Opposing ALLIANCE awarded PATTERN RP if G417.A
    G418 -- ROBOTS may not meddle with ARTIFACTS on RAMPS.
    Description ROBOTS may not contact, either directly or transitively through a SCORING ELEMENT CONTROLLED by the ROBOT, ARTIFACTS on a RAMP, including their own RAMP. Additionally, ROBOTS may not:.
  • A) remove an ARTIFACT from their own RAMP except by operating the GATE

    Description ROBOTS may not
  • A) intentionally place or LAUNCH ARTIFACTS directly onto their own RAMP
  • B) place or LAUNCH ARTIFACTS into the opponent’s GOAL or RAMP.

    • Intended scoring method is launching into the open top of your own GOAL.

    OPPONENT INTERACTION

    Note – G420 and G421 are mutually exclusive.
    A single ROBOT-to-ROBOT interaction that violates more than one of these rules results in only the most punitive penalty being assessed.
    G420 -- This is not combat robotics.
    Description A ROBOT may not deliberately functionally impair an opponent ROBOT.

    Damage or functional impairment because of contact with a tipped-over or DISABLED opponent ROBOT, which is not perceived by a REFEREE to be deliberate, is not a violation of this rule.
    Penalty Major Foul Yellow Card Major Foul + Red Card if opponent ROBOT is unable to drive
    G421 -- Do not tip or entangle.
    Description A ROBOT may not deliberately, as perceived by a REFEREE, attach to, tip, or entangle an opponent ROBOT.
    Penalty Major Foul Yellow Card Major Foul + Red Card if continuous or opponent ROBOT is unable to drive
    G422 -- There is a 3-count on PINS.
    Description A ROBOT may not PIN an opponent ROBOT for more than 3 seconds.

    A ROBOT is PINNING if it is preventing the movement of an opponent ROBOT by contact, either direct or transitive (such as against a FIELD element) and the opponent ROBOT is attempting to move.

    A PIN count ends once any of the following criteria are met:
    • A. The ROBOTS have separated by at least 2 ft. (~61 cm) from each other for more than 3 seconds.
    • B. Either ROBOT has moved 2 ft. from where the PIN initiated for more than 3 seconds.
    • C. The PINNING ROBOT gets PINNED.

    For criterion A, the PIN count pauses once ROBOTS are separated by 2 ft. until either the PIN ends or the PINNING ROBOT moves back within 2 ft., at which point the PIN count resumes.

    For criterion B, the PIN count pauses once either ROBOT has moved 2 ft. from where the PIN initiated until the PIN ends or until both ROBOTS move back within 2 ft., at which point the PIN count resumes.

    Penalty Minor Foul Additional Minor Foul for every 3 seconds not corrected
    G423 -- Do not use strategies intended to shut down major parts of gameplay.
    Description A ROBOT or ROBOTS may not, in the judgment of a REFEREE, isolate or close off any major element of MATCH play for a greater-than-MOMENTARY duration.

    Examples of violations include, but are not limited to:
    • A. Shutting down access to all SCORING ELEMENTS.
    • B. Quarantining an opponent to a small area of the FIELD.
    • C. Quarantining SCORING ELEMENTS out of the opposing ALLIANCE’S reach.
    • D. Completely blocking access to the opponent’s GATE.
    Penalty Minor Foul Additional Minor Foul for every 3 seconds not corrected
    G424 -- GATE ZONE is OFF LIMITS.
    Description A ROBOT may not contact, directly or transitively through a SCORING ELEMENT, an opponent ROBOT if either ROBOT is in the opponent’s GATE ZONE, regardless of who initiates contact.

    Exceptions:
    • A. A ROBOT in their own ALLIANCE’S GATE ZONE and in their opponent’s SECRET TUNNEL ZONE is not protected under G424.

    Note: For the exception in G424.A, G425 would apply instead.

    Penalty Minor Foul
    G425 -- Keep out of opponent’s SECRET TUNNEL.
    Description A ROBOT in the opponent’s SECRET TUNNEL ZONE may not contact, directly or transitively through a SCORING ELEMENT, an opponent ROBOT regardless of who initiates contact.
    Penalty Minor Foul
    G426 -- LOADING ZONE protection.
    Description A ROBOT may not contact, directly or transitively through a SCORING ELEMENT, an opponent ROBOT while either ROBOT is in the opponent’s LOADING ZONE, regardless of who initiates contact.
    Penalty Minor Foul
    G427 -- BASE ZONE protection.
    Description During the last 20 seconds of the MATCH, a ROBOT may not contact, directly or transitively through a SCORING ELEMENT, an opponent ROBOT while either ROBOT is in the opponent’s BASE ZONE, regardless of who initiates contact.
    Penalty Major Foul Opponent ROBOT (and any fully supported by opponent robot) awarded fully returned to BASE points

    HUMAN PLAYER

    G428 -- No wandering.
    Description DRIVE TEAM members must remain in their designated ALLIANCE AREA.
    • A. DRIVE TEAMS may be anywhere in their respective ALLIANCE AREA during a MATCH.
    • B. DRIVE TEAM members must be staged inside their respective ALLIANCE AREA prior to MATCH start.
    Penalty Verbal Warning Minor Foul if subsequent violations occur during the event
    Notes DRIVE TEAM members may retrieve SCORING ELEMENTS that have left the FIELD if they can do so without violating G428, G430, or G434. Reintroduction must follow G433.
    Exceptions are granted for safety or actions that are inadvertent, MOMENTARY, and inconsequential.
    G429 -- DRIVE COACHES and other teams: hands off the controls.
    Description A ROBOT shall be operated only by the DRIVERS of that team. DRIVE COACHES may not handle gamepads.

    DRIVE COACHES may assist the DRIVERS only in the following ways:
    • A. Holding the DRIVER STATION device
    • B. Troubleshooting the DRIVER STATION device
    • C. Selecting OpModes on the DRIVER STATION app
    • D. Pressing the INIT button on the DRIVER STATION app
    • E. Pressing the (▶) start button on the DRIVER STATION app
    • F. Pressing the (■) stop button on the DRIVER STATION app
    Penalty Major Foul Yellow Card if greater-than-momentary
    G430 -- DRIVE COACHES, SCORING ELEMENTS are off limits.
    Description DRIVE COACHES may not contact SCORING ELEMENTS, unless for safety purposes.
    Penalty Minor Foul
    G431 -- DRIVE TEAMS, watch your reach.
    Description Once a MATCH starts, a DRIVE TEAM member inside the FIELD may not:
    • A. Directly contact a ROBOT.
    • B. Contact a SCORING ELEMENT in contact with a ROBOT.
    • C. Disrupt SCORING ELEMENT scoring.
    • D. Contact a FIELD element.
    Penalty Major Foul + Yellow Card if G431.A Red Card + Opposing ALLIANCE awarded PATTERN RP if G431.C
    Notes Exceptions are granted for safety or actions that are inadvertent, MOMENTARY, and inconsequential.
    For G431.A, the penalty applies regardless of whether the DRIVE TEAM member or ROBOT initiates contact.

    Impacting ARTIFACT scoring includes:
    • A. Contacting an ARTIFACT LAUNCHED by the opponent within the FIELD.
    • B. Contacting an ARTIFACT in the opponent’s GOAL.
    • C. Disrupting the scoring of an ARTIFACT on the opponent’s RAMP or by operating the opponent’s GATE.
    • D. A DRIVE TEAM member LAUNCHING an ARTIFACT into a GOAL.
    G432 -- Humans, only meddle with ARTIFACTS in the LOADING ZONE.
    Description DRIVE TEAM members may only introduce ARTIFACTS to, remove ARTIFACTS from, or move ARTIFACTS within the LOADING ZONE and only the LOADING ZONE. Actions must occur:
    • A. only during TELEOP,
    • B. without using a tool,
    • C. without causing an ARTIFACT to enter into the LOADING ZONE from elsewhere on the FIELD, and
    • D. without causing an ARTIFACT to leave the LOADING ZONE and enter the rest of the FIELD unless the ARTIFACT is CONTROLLED by a ROBOT as follows:
      • I. ARTIFACT CONTROL begins when the ROBOT is in the LOADING ZONE, and
      • II. ARTIFACT is still CONTROLLED by the ROBOT when the ROBOT leaves the LOADING ZONE.
    Penalty Minor Foul per ARTIFACT Major Foul per ARTIFACT that enters top of GOAL
    G433 -- Humans may only enter SCORING ELEMENTS.
    Description DRIVE TEAM members may only enter ARTIFACTS onto the FIELD
    Penalty Minor Foul per non-ARTIFACT item entered onto the FIELD.
    G434 -- The ALLIANCE AREA has a storage limit.
    Description During TELEOP, each ALLIANCE may not store more than 6 ARTIFACTS out of play. DRIVE TEAM members making a good-faith effort to immediately re-enter additional ARTIFACTS into play are exempt.
    Penalty Minor Foul per ARTIFACT over the limit Additional Minor Foul per ARTIFACT over limit every 3 seconds until corrected
