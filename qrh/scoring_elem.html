G405 -- ROBOTS use SCORING ELEMENTS as directed.
Description A ROBOT may not deliberately use a SCORING ELEMENT to make a FIELD challenge easier or harder in a way not intended by the game design.
Penalty Major Foul per SCORING ELEMENT
Notes
  • Intentionally positioning SCORING ELEMENTS to block opponent access to FIELD elements.
  • Placing SCORING ELEMENTS into inaccessible areas (e.g., under the RAMP or GOAL).
  • Using SCORING ELEMENTS to prop open the GATE.
G406 -- Keep SCORING ELEMENTS in bounds.
Description A ROBOT may not intentionally eject a SCORING ELEMENT from the FIELD, whether directly or by bouncing off another object.
Penalty Major Foul per SCORING ELEMENT
Notes SCORING ELEMENTS that leave the FIELD during normal scoring attempts are not considered intentional ejections.
G407 -- Do not damage SCORING ELEMENTS.
Description Neither ROBOTS nor DRIVE TEAM members may damage SCORING ELEMENTS during play or handling.
Penalty Verbal Warning Major Foul if repeated Disabled if ROBOT damage likely to recur
Notes Corrective action (e.g., removing or modifying damaging mechanisms) and reinspection may be required before the ROBOT competes again.
G408 -- No more than 3 at a time.
Description A ROBOT may not simultaneously CONTROL more than three (3) ARTIFACTS.
Penalty Minor Foul per SCORING ELEMENT over the limit Yellow Card if excessive
Notes
  • Not considered CONTROL: bulldozing, deflecting, or incidental contact while collecting.
  • LAUNCHED elements no longer in contact with the ROBOT are not controlled.
  • Excessive control = 5+ ARTIFACTS at once or frequent violations (3+ occurrences with 4+ ARTIFACTS).
  • Design ROBOTS to prevent unintentional control above the limit.
